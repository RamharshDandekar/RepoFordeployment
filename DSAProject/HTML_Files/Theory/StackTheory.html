Stacks Concept

What are Stacks?

  • Imagine a pile of books, with books stacked one over the other. From this pile of books, you can either put another book on top or remove a book from the top.
  • The book which is at the bottom of the pile is the last one to be taken out, while the books at the top are removed first. Books can only be added to the top of the pile.
  • Let the action of putting a book on the top be called as push and let the action of removing a book be called pop. A structure similar to the example of the pile of books is known as a stack in computer science.

Stack Operations and Applications

Just like in the example of a stack of books, a stack data structure has two types of operations: push and pop. A stack follows the Last In, First Out (LIFO) principle, meaning the last element added is the first to be removed. Stacks have many applications:

  • Reversing a word: All the letters are pushed into the stack and then popped out one by one to reverse the word. This operation takes linear time O(n).
  • Undoing changes in a text editor: Changes made by the user are pushed into a stack. To undo, they are popped out.
